Sure, here is the Verilog-2001 module according to your requirements:

```verilog
module full_adder (input a, b, cin, output reg [1:0] out);
  assign out[0] = a ^ b ^ cin; // sum
  assign out[1] = (a & b) | (b & cin) | (a & cin); // cout
endmodule
```
In this module, `out` is an output port that is declared as a 2-bit wide wire. The first bit of `out` will be the sum and the second bit will be the carry out. This way you can use only continuous assign statements to calculate both outputs at once without using always blocks.
